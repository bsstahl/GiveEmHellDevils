Colin Linder

Posted: 2026-04-11

Colin Linder is back on the mound after redshirting 2025 while recovering from arm surgery. Before arriving in Tempe, he logged 16 appearances at Northwest Florida State with a 3.79 ERA and 50 strikeouts in 35.2 innings. The frame and the stuff are both intact, and the Devils are counting on that experience to surface quickly.

Jaxsyn Durst

Posted: 2026-04-03

A freshman outfielder from Reno, Jaxsyn Durst arrives in Tempe with the kind of profile that tends to age well in the Big‑12. A 2024 Northern 5A All‑League selection and finalist for Northern Nevada Player of the Year, he finished high school as a top‑10 outfielder in the state and a top‑500 national prospect. He also showed well on the summer circuit, earning a spot in the NNBCA Underclass All‑Star Game. It's still early in his Sun Devil career, but the tools and competitiveness are there for him to grow into a meaningful role in the years ahead.

Don't Pitch to Hairston if You Want to Win

Posted: 2026-03-31

Landon Hairston has been hitting like a video‑game create‑a-player: a .468 average with a 1.544 OPS and 15 home runs in 28 games is production that almost never shows up in real life. These numbers aren't just unbelievable, they're the kind of once‑in‑a‑lifetime statistics that makes you double‑check the stat sheet to be sure it isn’t a typo. We are witnessing something special for Sun Devil baseball in 2026.

A senior left‑hander who has become one of ASU's most reliable late‑inning options, trusted to take on the tougher pockets of a lineup and work through the moments that matter. His track record of handling inherited runners and navigating leverage has made him a go‑to arm in tight situations.

Zavorek Set and Ready

Posted: 2026-03-19

Beckett Zavorek has settled in as a reliable infield presence, showing the same quick transfer and clean defensive instincts that defined his Bullard High days. He’s already stacked a pair of multi‑hit games this season and continues to put together competitive at‑bats. A lean, athletic sophomore who plays with quiet confidence, he looks more comfortable every week.

Brody Briggs, Redshirt Sophomore

Posted: 2026-03-12

Redshirt sophomore catcher Brody Briggs gets another look at TCU this weekend, a year after he stunned them in Fort Worth with his first two collegiate homers — a three‑run shot and a grand slam out of the nine‑hole for seven RBIs in the 12–11 win. About this site

Give 'Em Hell Devils! is an amateur photoblog by and for fans of ASU Sun Devil Baseball. Our favorite photos of Sun Devil players and coaches can be found right here. They are put here entirely for the enjoyment of Sun Devil fans.

This site is not affiliated with Arizona State University or ASU Sun Devil Baseball. All opinions and editorial decisions are the responsibility of the site operators and not of the University or the baseball program.

About the Editor

Barry S. Stahl is a Software Engineer who has been taking photos at Sun Devil baseball games since the late 1990s. Aside from watching baseball and building software, Barry also enjoys hockey and is an Election Integrity Activist, husband of one genius and father of another, and a 40+ year resident of Phoenix Arizona. When he is not traveling around the world to speak at Technical Conferences or sitting in the stands at Phoenix Municipal Stadium, he can be found designing software intensive systems for AZNerds.net in Tempe AZ or working on the next AZGiveCamp event, where software creators come together to build websites and apps for some great non-profit organizations.
